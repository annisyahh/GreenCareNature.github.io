The avocado hass picked directly from the tree, this fruit is very popular in the United States because it contains vitamins A, D, and E, and the fat in avocados is proven to be able to hydrate the skin so that it makes the skin moist. In addition, using avocado oil on the skin can also prevent sun damage, soothe inflamed skin, and promote healing of injured skin. The linoleic and oleic acids in avocados also help repair.

Kyuri Cucumber from Japan is very popular in the skin care industry. Not infrequently people make this fruit into the basic ingredients of skin care products. The center of the cucumber contains lots of water, vitamin C, vitamin A, folate and caffeic acid which have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. This is why cucumbers are often thinly sliced and applied to tired faces or eyes. The content of folate and vitamin C in cucumber is thought to reduce swelling, and make the eyes look and feel fresh.

Pu Erh Green Tea, which is imported directly from China, contains polyphenols and six different types of catechins, including epigallocatechin gallate (EGCG) and epicatechin gallate (ECG). Both compounds are antioxidants that can prevent cancer. The antioxidant EGCG has the ability to rejuvenate aging skin cells. Antioxidant compounds are known to combat signs of aging and make skin look healthier and brighter. Green tea also contains vitamin E which helps nourish and hydrate the skin.
